Afang Soup

75 mins
169 calories

A traditional Nigerian soup adapted with plant-based proteins, seasonal greens, and sustainable practices for an eco-conscious twist.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (or substitute with spinach or lamb’s lettuce) Afang leaves
  • 2 cups (or substitute with amaranth or moringa) waterleaf
  • 2 tablespoons (sustainably sourced, or use olive oil) palm oil
  • 1, chopped (local and in season) onion
  • 2 cloves, minced garlic
  • 1 small, chopped (or other seasonal peppers) bell pepper
  • 1-2, blended (or canned if fresh is unavailable) tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon (optional, or substitute with nutritional yeast) ground crayfish
  • 1 teaspoon (or to taste) ground pepper
  • 1 teaspoon (or fresh if available) dried thyme
  • 1-2 cups (preferably homemade) vegetable broth
  • 1 cup (black-eyed peas, kidney beans, or chickpeas) cooked beans
  • 1/2 cup (optional, for protein and texture) tofu
  • to taste salt
  • 1/4 cup (optional, parsley or cilantro for garnish) fresh herbs
  • 1/4 cup (optional, for added richness) roasted peanuts or groundnut paste

Instructions

  1. Wash and chop the Afang leaves and waterleaf (or substitute greens) into smaller pieces. Set them aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat palm oil (or olive oil) over medium heat. Sauté onions and garlic for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  3. Add chopped bell pepper and cook for another 2 minutes. Stir in blended tomatoes, ground crayfish (or nutritional yeast), ground pepper, and thyme. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the tomato mixture thickens and oil rises to the top.
  4. Add cooked beans and tofu to the pot, stirring to combine. If using roasted peanuts or groundnut paste, add them now. Cook for an additional 5 minutes to blend flavors.
  5. Pour in vegetable broth and simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the soup to thicken and flavors to meld.
  6. Add chopped Afang leaves and waterleaf. Stir well and cook for 5-7 minutes until greens are tender.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or more broth as needed.
  8. Serve hot with rice, fufu, or any preferred side dish.

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Plant-Based Protein: Beans and tofu replace meat or fish, reducing the environmental footprint.
  • Local and Seasonal Ingredients: Greens like spinach or amaranth reduce food miles and support local farming.
  • Homemade Broth: Encourages making broth from kitchen scraps to reduce packaging waste.
  • Sustainable Palm Oil: Suggests sustainably sourced palm oil or olive oil to minimize environmental impact.
  • Waste Reduction: Promotes using leftover vegetables or beans, helping reduce food waste.
  • Minimal Packaging: Focuses on fresh, local ingredients to avoid heavily packaged products.
