Pasta

30 mins
131 calories

Here’s a sustainable Pasta recipe that focuses on plant-based ingredients, minimizes food waste, and uses locally sourced and seasonal vegetables to create a flavorful dish. This recipe is simple, healthy, and reduces environmental impact while still being delicious. Sustainable Pasta Primavera with Seasonal Vegetables

Ingredients

  • 8 oz (about 225g) whole wheat pasta
  • 1 tablespoon, extra virgin, sustainably sourced olive oil
  • 1, sliced (or any seasonal squash) zucchini
  • 1 cup, halved (or other in-season tomatoes) cherry tomatoes
  • 1, chopped bell pepper
  • 1 small bunch, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces (or green beans) asparagus
  • 2 cloves, minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon (or zest for more flavor) lemon juice
  • to taste salt
  • to taste pepper
  • chopped (for garnish) basil or parsley
  • 1/4 cup (optional, for a cheesy flavor) nutritional yeast
  • 1/4 cup (optional) Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup (optional for added crunch) roasted sunflower seeds or pine nuts

Instructions

  1. Cook the whole wheat pasta in a large pot of salted water until al dente (about 9-12 minutes).
  2. Drain the pasta, reserving 1/2 cup of the pasta water. Set the pasta aside.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add zucchini, bell pepper, and asparagus (or green beans). Sauté for 5-7 minutes until tender but crisp.
  4. Add cherry tomatoes and garlic, cooking for another 2 minutes until tomatoes soften.
  5. Add the drained pasta to the skillet with vegetables. Toss to combine, adding the reserved pasta water to help the sauce come together.
  6. Stir in lemon juice (or zest) and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Remove from heat and toss in fresh basil or parsley. Optionally, add nutritional yeast or Parmesan, and top with roasted sunflower seeds or pine nuts.
  8. Serve warm.

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Whole Wheat Pasta: Whole wheat pasta is a healthier option with more fiber and nutrients. It’s also typically produced with less processing than white pasta, making it more environmentally friendly.
  • Seasonal Vegetables: This dish uses seasonal and local vegetables like zucchini, cherry tomatoes, bell peppers, and asparagus. Choosing seasonal produce helps reduce food miles and supports local farming.
  • Plant-Based: By using only vegetables, this pasta dish is entirely plant-based, which generally has a smaller environmental footprint compared to meat-based dishes.
  • Minimal Food Waste: The recipe is flexible, allowing you to use whatever vegetables are in season or leftover from other meals. You can also repurpose vegetable scraps (e.g., stems, peels) into homemade vegetable broth for future use.
  • Sustainable Olive Oil: Using olive oil from sustainably sourced producers supports environmentally friendly farming practices.
