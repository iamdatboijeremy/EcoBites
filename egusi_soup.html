Egusi Soup

80 mins
182 calories

A rich, plant-based Nigerian soup made with egusi seeds, seasonal vegetables, and sustainable ingredients, perfect for an eco-conscious meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (locally sourced or repurposed from leftover seeds) ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 2 tablespoons (sustainably sourced, or substitute with olive oil) palm oil
  • 1, chopped (local and in season) onion
  • 2 cloves, minced garlic
  • 1 small, chopped (or other seasonal vegetables) bell pepper
  • 1-2, chopped (fresh or canned) tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon (optional, or substitute with nutritional yeast) ground crayfish
  • 2 cups (homemade or low-sodium) vegetable broth
  • 1 cup (or other leafy greens like kale) spinach
  • 1/2 cup (frozen or fresh, or substitute with green beans, okra, or zucchini) peas
  • 1 teaspoon (dried or fresh if available) thyme
  • 1 teaspoon (or to taste) ground pepper
  • to taste salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon (optional) ground ginger
  • 1/2 cup (optional, for added protein) cooked beans

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, mix the ground egusi with about 1/4 cup of warm water to form a thick paste. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the palm oil (or olive oil) over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the chopped bell pepper and tomatoes. Stir and cook for 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes release their juices.
  4. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Add the ground crayfish (or nutritional yeast) for umami flavor. Season with thyme, ground pepper, and salt. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Stir the egusi paste into the soup. Cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the soup thickens.
  6. Add the leafy greens, peas, and other seasonal vegetables. Stir and cook for 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  7. If using cooked beans, stir them in at this stage to add protein and texture.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or additional spices as needed.
  9. Serve the soup warm with sustainable side dishes like brown rice, fufu, or pounded yam.

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Locally Sourced Ingredients: Supports local farming communities and reduces food miles by using local produce like spinach and kale.
  • Plant-Based Option: Reduces environmental impact while maintaining a healthy, protein-rich profile with vegetables, beans, and egusi.
  • Homemade Broth: Encourages using kitchen scraps to reduce waste and avoid packaging.
  • Minimal Waste: Adapts to seasonal ingredients and leftovers, minimizing food waste.
  • Sustainable Palm Oil: Recommends sustainably sourced palm oil to prevent deforestation and biodiversity loss, with olive oil as an alternative.
  • Flexible Ingredients: Promotes sustainability by using whatever seasonal vegetables are available, reducing food waste and ensuring adaptability.
