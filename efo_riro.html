Efo Riro

60 mins
177 calories

A savory, plant-based Nigerian spinach stew made with seasonal vegetables, plant-based proteins, and sustainable ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (or local leafy greens like kale or collard greens) fresh spinach
  • 1 tablespoon (sustainably sourced, or substitute with olive oil) palm oil
  • 1, chopped (local and in season) onion
  • 2 cloves, minced garlic
  • 1 small, chopped (or other seasonal peppers) bell pepper
  • 2-3, blended (or canned if fresh is unavailable) fresh tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon (optional, or substitute with nutritional yeast) ground crayfish
  • 1 teaspoon (or fresh if available) dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon (or to taste) ground pepper
  • 1 teaspoon (optional) ground ginger
  • 2 cups (homemade or low-sodium) vegetable broth
  • 1 cup (optional, for protein; black-eyed peas, kidney beans, or chickpeas work well) cooked beans
  • 1 tablespoon (low-sodium option) soy sauce or tamari
  • to taste salt
  • 1/2 cup (optional, or use other plant-based protein like tempeh) tofu
  • 1/4 cup (optional, parsley or cilantro for garnish) fresh herbs

Instructions

  1. Wash and chop the spinach (or other leafy greens). Remove stems if using tougher greens like kale.
  2. In a large pot, heat the palm oil (or olive oil) over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and garlic, cooking for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the chopped bell pepper and cook for 2 minutes. Stir in the blended tomatoes, thyme, ground pepper, ginger, and soy sauce. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the tomato mixture thickens and oil floats to the top.
  4. Add crumbled tofu or cooked beans to the pot. Stir to combine and cook for 5-7 minutes to blend the flavors.
  5. Pour in the vegetable broth, stir, and let simmer for 10 minutes to allow the stew to thicken slightly.
  6. Add the chopped spinach (or other leafy greens) and stir well. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the greens are wilted and the stew reaches your desired consistency.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or soy sauce as needed.
  8. Serve hot with rice, fufu, or your preferred side dish.

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Plant-Based Protein: Using tofu or beans as a protein source reduces the environmental impact compared to animal proteins, while maintaining a nutrient-rich meal.
  • Seasonal and Local Ingredients: Incorporates seasonal vegetables like spinach or kale, supporting local farming and reducing food miles.
  • Homemade Broth: Encourages making vegetable broth from kitchen scraps to minimize packaging waste and enhance sustainability.
  • Minimized Waste: Adapts to available leftovers (e.g., cooked greens, beans) and promotes reducing food waste.
  • Sustainable Palm Oil: Suggests sustainably sourced palm oil or olive oil to minimize deforestation and biodiversity impact.
  • Minimal Packaging: Focuses on fresh vegetables and homemade components to reduce reliance on packaged items.
