Pilaf

35 mins
359 calories

A Sustainable Vegetable Pilaf using whole grains, seasonal vegetables, and plant-based ingredients to create a flavorful and eco-friendly dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (or any locally sourced whole grain like quinoa, farro, or barley) brown rice
  • 2 tablespoons (extra virgin and sustainably sourced) olive oil
  • 1, chopped (local and in season) onion
  • 2 cloves, minced garlic
  • 1, diced (or any seasonal root vegetable) carrot
  • 1, diced (or other seasonal vegetables like zucchini or eggplant) bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup (fresh or frozen, or any other green veggie like green beans) peas
  • 1 teaspoon (dried or fresh if available) thyme
  • 1 teaspoon (or another preferred spice like coriander or turmeric) ground cumin
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cups (preferably homemade or low-sodium) vegetable broth
  • to taste salt and pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped (like parsley, cilantro, or mint) fresh herbs
  • Optional (like sunflower seeds, almonds, or pine nuts) toasted nuts or seeds

Instructions

  1. Rinse the brown rice under cold water to remove excess starch. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the garlic, carrots, and bell pepper (or other seasonal vegetables). Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are softened and slightly caramelized.
  4. Add the thyme, cumin, bay leaf, salt, and pepper to the vegetables. Stir to coat the vegetables in the spices, and let the spices cook for a minute to enhance their aroma.
  5. Add the rinsed rice to the pot, stirring to mix it with the vegetables and spices.
  6. Pour in the vegetable broth, bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and simmer for 35-40 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid has been absorbed. Adjust cooking time for different grains as needed.
  7. About 5 minutes before the rice is done cooking, stir in the peas (or other green vegetables). Cover again and allow them to steam until the rice is cooked through.
  8. Once the rice is cooked, remove the pot from heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes. Remove the bay leaf and fluff the rice with a fork.
  9. Stir in the chopped fresh herbs and adjust seasoning if needed.
  10. Serve the pilaf warm as a main dish or a side. Garnish with toasted nuts or seeds for added texture.

Why This Recipe is Sustainable

  • Whole Grains: Using whole grains like brown rice, quinoa, or barley provides more nutrients and is less processed, reducing environmental impact.
  • Seasonal and Local Vegetables: This recipe adapts to what’s in season and local, lowering food miles and supporting local farming.
  • Plant-Based: Entirely plant-based, this dish has a smaller carbon footprint than meat-based dishes.
  • Homemade Broth: Using homemade vegetable broth from scraps reduces waste and avoids packaged alternatives.
  • Minimal Waste: Encourages using leftovers and seasonal produce to reduce food waste.
  • Energy Efficient Cooking: Brown rice, when cooked with the lid on, retains heat well, reducing energy usage.
